Working with us as a subject-matter expert

v1.1 · June 24, 2026 You have been asked to lend your expertise to one lesson. This page explains why, what we ask of you, and how your answers become a full AI literacy lesson.
Why this exists. The Future Ready AI Collaborative, a grant based at the Naveen Jindal School of Management (UT Dallas), builds ready-to-teach AI literacy lessons. They are used to teach high school students (grades 10 to 12) and the first-year university students they become. We need genuine expertise on each topic, and we want it to be a light lift on you. So your knowledge of one lesson, given once, becomes a full class: our design team, with Microsoft Copilot (the university's institutionally approved AI), does the building, and you review and approve everything. What you share in the intake feeds the 17 deliverables that become the lesson, the part you will see on the next two tabs.

Why it works. One submission from you produces every deliverable; your time goes only where expertise is irreplaceable, supplying the knowledge and validating the result. The same lesson then serves grades 10, 11, and 12 from your single intake.

How you give us the content, three ways

Every path feeds the same 17 deliverables and the same approval loop. Nothing ships without your sign-off.

1 · Answer the questions

About 15 short prompts in a Microsoft Form, on your own schedule, 60 to 90 minutes.

2 · Just send materials

Hand us a lesson plan, slides, notes, or a recorded class, and answer nothing. We mine them and draft the answers; you do a quick confirm-and-correct pass.

3 · Both (recommended)

Answer what you like and attach your materials. The questions capture your intent; the documents become cited references and seed the AI tutor.

What your input becomes, the 17 deliverables

You never build these. You answer about 15 questions; our design team, with Microsoft Copilot, expands them into all 17 elements, and you approve every gate. Here is who does what.

Foundation

  • 1 Learning outcome you name the capability
  • 2 Key concepts you give the 3 to 5 ideas + the misconceptions
  • 3 Vocabulary you give the domain terms; we tier them
  • 4 Standards our task; you provide nothing

Learner materials

  • 5 Student-facing lesson you give the substance + a worked example
  • 6 Concept videos ×2 we script from your key concepts
  • 7 Scenario video you give one real field moment
  • 8 In-class activity you say what practice and failure look like
  • 9 Going-deeper you give the one "most people don't know" insight
  • 10 Extension we derive from the real-life connection

Teacher enablement

  • 11 Teacher Guide you give the misconceptions, the why, the pitfalls
  • 12 Teacher video we build from the guide

Assessment & data

  • 13 Formative assessment you give one check idea + the misconceptions
  • 14 Answer key / rubric we build, you confirm
  • 15 Pre-assessment we mirror the formative banks

Supports & future

  • 16 Discussion scaffolds we build talking points + sentence stems
  • 17 AI-tutor knowledge base we chunk + embed; you check accuracy
You do not worry about how it lands in the lesson plan. Our design team maps every deliverable into the right grade's lesson format and reading level. Your focus is only what you provide, which is the intake on the previous tab.
