The 17 deliverables, and who does what

The expert never builds these. They answer about 15 questions; the design team and AI expand the answers into all 17, and the expert approves every gate. The full element definitions are on the grade pages (shared with everyone); this is the at-a-glance "what the expert provides."

Foundation

  • 1 Learning outcome expert names the capability
  • 2 Key concepts expert gives the 3 to 5 ideas + the misconceptions
  • 3 Vocabulary expert gives the domain terms; we tier them
  • 4 Standards design-team task; expert provides nothing

Scholar materials

  • 5 Student-facing lesson expert gives substance + a worked example
  • 6 Concept videos ×2 we script from the key concepts
  • 7 Scenario video expert gives one real field moment
  • 8 In-class activity expert says what practice and failure look like
  • 9 Going-deeper expert gives the one "most people don't know" insight
  • 10 Extension we derive from the real-life connection

Teacher enablement

  • 11 Teacher Guide expert gives misconceptions, the why, pitfalls
  • 12 Teacher video we build from the guide

Assessment & data

  • 13 Formative assessment expert gives one check idea + the misconceptions
  • 14 Answer key / rubric we build, expert confirms
  • 15 Pre-assessment we mirror the formative banks

Supports & future

  • 16 Discussion scaffolds we build talking points + sentence stems
  • 17 AI-tutor knowledge base we chunk + embed; expert checks accuracy
The expert does not care how it lands in the partner's lesson plan. That mapping (Tab ① of each grade page) is the design team's job. The expert cares about what they provide and how, which is the intake on the next tab.

How the expert gives us the content · three ways

Every path feeds the same 17 deliverables and the same approval loop. Nothing ships without the expert's sign-off.

1 · Answer the questions

About 15 short prompts in a Microsoft Form, async, on the expert's own schedule (60 to 90 minutes).

2 · Just send materials

Hand us a lesson plan, decks, notes, or a recorded class, and answer nothing. We mine them and draft the answers; the expert does a quick confirm-and-correct pass.

3 · Both (recommended)

Answer what you like and attach materials. The questions capture intent; the documents become cited source references and seed the AI tutor.
