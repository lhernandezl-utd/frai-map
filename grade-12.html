TOK Year 2 (Grade 12) · Lesson plan Anchor: "Knowledge and technology" · placement: early Year 2 (senior fall), as the Prescribed Title work begins
Unit / Anchor text
Knowledge and technology: Can AI produce knowledge? Bias and the reliability of AI in research
Objective SWBAT…
Scholars will be able to analyze how bias enters an AI system (data, design, deployment) and evaluate when AI is an acceptable research aid for the Prescribed Title Essay, disclosed and cited, and when it is not.
Why It Matters
Year 2 writes the Essay. AI can help pressure-test an argument, but the IB requires it to be disclosed and cited, and the partner does not allow it to generate submission text. Knowing the line protects the diploma.
IB Framework
Conceptual / Knowledge Question: To what extent is interpretation a reliable tool in the production of knowledge? (applied to AI)
Learner Profile: Principled · Reflective
ATLs: Thinking (critical) · Research · Self-management
Key Points
  • Bias enters AI through its data, its design, and how it is deployed.
  • "The data does not lie" is a myth; data carries the past.
  • AI is a research aid, not an author.
  • The IB requires disclosure and citation of AI use, including the prompt and the date; verify every claim.
Central Vocabulary / Command Term
Command terms: evaluate · justify · discuss. AI AI terms: algorithmic bias · training data · hallucination · disclosure / citation.
Potential Misunderstandings
  • "Using AI at all is cheating." Disclosed, cited use as a thinking aid is permitted; generating submission text is not.
  • "A computer is neutral." Bias can enter at several stages.
Do First · Do Now 10 min
ROUTINE The Year 2 argument-building opener. AI Scholars read a short AI-generated argument and look for a hidden bias or an unsupported claim.
Introduction to New Material & Teacher Model 15 min · I do
AI Scenario clip of a real biased-AI case. The teacher models the IB-compliant way to use AI in research: disclose it, cite the prompt and date, then verify each claim against two independent sources.
Guided Practice 10 min · we do
ROUTINE The prescribed-title "speed dating" discussion. AI In rotating pairs, scholars map where bias entered a sample AI output and which area of knowledge it touches.
Independent Practice 5 min · you do
AI Exit-ticket write: one way AI could ethically help with your Prescribed Title Essay, and one way it could not.
Formative Assessment / Exit Ticket
Annotate one prescribed title; mark where a disclosed, cited AI aid could help and where it would cross the line; draft one knowledge question linking AI bias to an area of knowledge. Exemplar in the Teacher Guide.
Feeds the IB-graded pieces IB
Essay: directly strengthens prescribed-title research with cited, verification-first practice. Academic integrity: establishes the disclosure-and-citation habit the partner requires. Supports the Essay; never generates it.

What each of the 17 elements IS, what the expert provides, and what the design team builds. This is for everyone, the partner and the expert alike. The elements are the same for every grade.

Reading-level rule: scholar-facing prose at grade 7 to 9 readability; every video captioned; academic vocabulary taught with cognates for emergent bilingual scholars.

Foundation

1 · Learning outcome one observable sentence

What a scholar can do after the lesson, in observable verbs. The expert names the capability; the design team phrases it and aligns the assessment.

2 · Key concepts the 3 to 5 ideas that must survive

Each maps to an assessment item and usually a video. The expert provides the concepts and the misconceptions that fight them. In TOK these become a knowledge question.

3 · Vocabulary, Beck's three tiers EB instruction standard

Tier 1 everyday · Tier 2 academic words (with cognates) · Tier 3 domain terms. Focus on Tiers 2 and 3; AI terms sit beside the IB command terms.

4 · Standards mapping one code per lesson

The standard the lesson satisfies. For TOK, alignment to the existing TOK course (TEKS being confirmed). Design-team task.

Scholar materials

5 · Student-facing content

Hook → concept (with video) → guided try → independent try → close. The expert supplies substance and a worked example; AI drafts to the audience; the expert signs off.

6 · Concept videos ×2

One short captioned video per key-concept cluster: one idea, one analogy. The videos carry the teaching.

7 · Scenario video

The idea working or failing in a real moment. In TOK it also seeds a knowledge question.

8 · In-class activity

Hands-on practice: stump it · catch it · fix it · judge it. The expert says what practice and what failure look like.

9 · Going-deeper segment

The knowledge-question debate that feeds the Essay and Exhibition. The expert provides the one insight they wish scholars knew.

10 · Extension activity

A deeper optional task for scholars who want more.

Teacher enablement

11 · Teacher Guide

Makes the lesson teachable by a non-expert. The expert provides the misconceptions, the why, and the pitfalls.

12 · Teacher video

The guide's elevator version, watched once before teaching.

Assessment, supports & future

13 · Formative assessment

Built to auto-grade where possible; every item maps to a key concept. The expert provides one assessment idea and the misconceptions that become distractors.

14 · Answer key / rubric

Scoring and which misses signal which reteach. Lives in the Teacher Guide.

15 · Pre-assessment / baseline

Short baseline before the unit; mirrors the formative banks. Runs in the partner's systems; the university receives de-identified aggregates.

16 · Discussion scaffolds

Teacher talking points + scholar sentence stems, bilingual-friendly, using Tier 2 vocabulary.

17 · AI-tutor knowledge base

Content distilled into retrievable chunks so the future tutor answers grounded in approved content. Internal until the tutor ships.

Academic integrity note. Across TOK we teach AI as a disclosed, cited research aid, never to generate IB submission text. The deployment platform carries the lessons; it does not change that guardrail.

The proposed 12th-grade curriculum (TOK): 4 units, 8 lessons, one semester, standalone senior overviews. 12th grade is senior fall only (spring is taken by the Extended Essay, CAS, and exams), so it is condensed; the AI segment runs about 2 weeks. Lessons run about 45 minutes. This is a projected draft for your feedback. Click a lesson to expand.

Unit 1 · AI Foundations (Condensed)

Lessons 1 to 2
1 · What Is AI + How Machines Learn
EQ: What is AI and how does it learn? Flow: hook (10) · definitions + ML basics lecture (25) · pattern activity (15) · exit ticket (5).
2 · Generative AI Decoded
EQ: How do tools like ChatGPT create content? Flow: walkthrough (15) · hands-on exploration (25) · discussion (15).

Unit 2 · Using AI Responsibly

Lessons 3 to 4
3 · Prompt Engineering: Using AI Effectively
EQ: How do we get the best results from AI? Flow: demo (10) · guided lab (30) · challenge + share (15).
4 · When AI Gets It Wrong: Hallucinations, Bias & Privacy
EQ: How do we use AI safely and critically? Flow: spot-the-error (15) · bias + privacy lecture (25) · checklist creation (15).

Unit 3 · AI Ethics & Society

Lessons 5 to 6
5 · Who's Responsible? Accountability & Academic Integrity
EQ: Who is accountable for AI decisions and honest use? Flow: scenario debate (20) · framework + integrity lecture (20) · reflection (15).
6 · Deepfakes, Synthetic Media & AI Authorship
EQ: Can we trust what we see, and who owns AI work? Flow: detection activity (20) · authorship lecture (20) · discussion (15).

Unit 4 · AI in the Real World & Your Future

Lessons 7 to 8
7 · AI Across Industries & the Future of Work
EQ: How will AI shape industries and careers? Flow: industry examples (20) · mini-lecture (20) · skills mapping (15).
8 · The Road Ahead: Emerging Trends & Your Role
EQ: How do I thrive in an AI-driven future? Flow: trends lecture (20) · future-of-me reflection (20) · course synthesis + share (15).
